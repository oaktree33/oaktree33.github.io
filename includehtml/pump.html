Peristaltic Pump Project

Objective:

The goal of this project was to develop a low-budget peristaltic pump for the application of our choice.
We chose design and prototype a peristaltic pump for gastric feeding.

Design Contraints:

Below is the list of typical contraints for gastric feeding pump:

  • Flow Rate
    • Increment of 0.1ml/hr from 0.1 ml/hr to 10ml/hr
    • Error needs to be within 5 percent.
  • Compatibility
    • The tubing used must be compatible with the standardized feeding solutions.
    • Must be biocompatible with various vitamins, proteins, glucose, and lipids.
    • Feeding solutions are usually at least 70% water.
  • Requires daily replacement of tubing by the nurses or therapists.

Resource Contraints:

  • Budget: $100
  • Material Processng tools: Laser Cutter, and Fusion Deposit Modeller.
  • Building Materials: Acrylic sheets, and Acrylonitrile butadiene styrene(ABS)

Final Concept Design:

Below was the final concept design:

Each layer is an acrylic sheet. The thickness of the pump case, and the rotary assembly has been dimensioned to accomodate the chosen tubing and the desired flow rates.
The Tygon S-50-HL of 1/8 inch tubing was chosen for its 25-30 hour life and its slow flow rate (0.8cc per inch).
The inner circuilar plate and the associated rotary part can be easily slide out to facilitate tube replacement.

Below is a short video of prototype demonstration:

The motor and gearbox assembly that we used was purchased from Tamiya.
The motor had the RPM of 6500, and the gear ratio that we chose was 1300:1. Such gear ratio was needed in order to achieve minimum flow rate of 0.1 ml per hour.

Following video is a quick demonstration of the speed control by using pulse width modulation from a microcontroller.

As you can see in the video, the gearbox and the rotary assembly can simply slide out of the outer casing.
